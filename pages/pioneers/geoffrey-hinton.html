Geoffrey Hinton

Godfather of Deep Learning

Biography

Geoffrey Everest Hinton (born 1947) is a British-Canadian cognitive psychologist and computer scientist, most renowned for his work on artificial neural networks. He is one of the pioneers of deep learning and won the 2018 Turing Award (alongside Yann LeCun and Yoshua Bengio).

Key Contributions

1986: Backpropagation

Published "Learning Representations by Back-propagating Errors" with David Rumelhart and Ronald Williams, popularizing backpropagation for training neural networks.

2006: Deep Learning

Published "A Fast Learning Algorithm for Deep Belief Nets," introducing efficient training of deep neural networks and coining the term "deep learning."

2012: AlexNet

His students Alex Krizhevsky and Ilya Sutskever built AlexNet, which won the ImageNet competition and triggered the deep learning revolution.

Current Work

Hinton continues to work on neural network architectures and has expressed concerns about the future of AI safety. He joined Google Brain in 2013 and was Vice President and Fellow at Google.
