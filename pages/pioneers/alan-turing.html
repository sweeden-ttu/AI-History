Alan Turing

The Father of Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence

Biography

Alan Mathison Turing (1912-1954) was a British mathematician, computer scientist, logician, cryptanalyst, philosopher, and theoretical biologist. He is widely considered to be the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.

Key Contributions

1936: Turing Machine

Published "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the Turing machine - the theoretical foundation of modern computing.

1950: Turing Test

Published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the famous Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence.

1950: CAPTCHA

Introduced the concept of the "imitation game," which evolved into modern CAPTCHA tests used to distinguish humans from computers.

Legacy

Turing's work established the fundamental questions that still drive AI research today:

  • Can machines think?
  • What is intelligence?
  • Can computers match human cognition?

The Turing Award, computing's highest honor, is named in his honor.
