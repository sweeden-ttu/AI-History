Fei-Fei Li

Pioneer of Computer Vision and AI for Good

Biography

Fei-Fei Li (born 1976) is a Chinese-American computer scientist specializing in computer vision and cognitive neuroscience. She is a professor at Stanford University and was the director of Stanford's AI Lab and Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute.

Key Contributions

2007: ImageNet

Conceived and led the creation of ImageNet, a massive visual database with over 14 million labeled images that revolutionized computer vision research.

2010-2017: ImageNet Challenge

The annual ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) pushed the boundaries of computer vision, with deep learning eventually surpassing human accuracy.

AI for Good

Founded AI4ALL, a nonprofit increasing diversity in AI through education, and advocates for human-centered AI development.

Current Work

Li continues at Stanford focusing on AI for healthcare, robotics, and human-centered AI. She serves on the board of Twitter (X) and various tech companies.
