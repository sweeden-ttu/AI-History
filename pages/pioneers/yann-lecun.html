Yann LeCun

Pioneer of Convolutional Neural Networks

Biography

Yann LeCun (born 1960) is a French computer scientist focusing on convolutional neural networks. He is the director of AI research at Facebook (Meta) and a professor at NYU. He won the 2018 Turing Award with Geoffrey Hinton and Yoshua Bengio.

Key Contributions

1989: LeNet

Developed LeNet, one of the first convolutional neural networks, used for handwritten digit recognition (ZIP code reading for USPS).

1998: LeNet-5

Published the seminal "Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition" paper, establishing the modern CNN architecture.

2013: Facebook AI

Became the founding director of Facebook AI Research (FAIR), leading AI research at Meta.

Current Work

LeCun continues to push AI research at Meta, focusing on self-supervised learning, AI reasoning, and hardware optimization.
