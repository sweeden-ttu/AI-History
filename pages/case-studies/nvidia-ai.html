NVIDIA

GPU Computing and the AI Revolution

The NVIDIA Story

NVIDIA transformed from a gaming graphics company into the backbone of the AI revolution, with its GPUs powering everything from ChatGPT to autonomous vehicles.

Key Milestones

1993: Founded

Jensen Huang founds NVIDIA to build graphics processors for gaming and multimedia.

2006: CUDA

NVIDIA launches CUDA, enabling general-purpose computing on GPUs - a pivotal moment for AI.

2012: AI Breakthrough

AlexNet on NVIDIA GPUs wins ImageNet competition, triggering the deep learning boom.

2018: Tensor Cores

Introduction of Tensor Cores specifically designed for deep learning acceleration.

2020: Data Centers

NVIDIA Data Center revenue surpasses gaming, driven by AI demand.

2024: Blackwell

Blackwell architecture pushes AI computing to new levels with massive model support.

NVIDIA's AI Ecosystem

  • Hardware: A100, H100, Blackwell GPUs
  • Software: CUDA, cuDNN, TensorRT
  • Cloud: NVIDIA DGX Cloud
  • AI Platforms: NVIDIA AI Enterprise
