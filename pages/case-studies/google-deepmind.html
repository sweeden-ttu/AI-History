Google DeepMind

From AlphaGo to Protein Folding

The DeepMind Story

DeepMind, acquired by Google in 2014, has become one of the world's leading AI research labs, known for breakthrough achievements in reinforcement learning and protein structure prediction.

Key Milestones

2010: Founded

Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa Suleyman found DeepMind in London with a focus on AGI.

2014: Acquired by Google

Google acquires DeepMind for approximately $500 million, providing massive computational resources.

2016: AlphaGo

AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol, world Go champion, demonstrating superhuman decision-making.

2020: AlphaFold

AlphaFold solves the protein folding problem, a 50-year grand challenge in biology.

2022: AlphaFold Database

DeepMind releases 200 million protein structures, freely available to scientists worldwide.

2024: Project Astra

DeepMind unveils general-purpose AI assistants, pushing toward more capable AI systems.

Impact Areas

  • Science: Materials science, physics research
  • Gaming: Game AI, strategy optimization
  • General AI: Toward more capable, general systems
