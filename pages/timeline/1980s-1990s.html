1980s-1990s: Expert Systems Era

AI finds business applications and machine learning begins to emerge

1980: Expert Systems

AI finds real-world business applications with expert systems like MYCIN for medical diagnosis and XCON for computer configuration.

1986: Backpropagation Revival

Geoffrey Hinton and colleagues publish "Learning Representations by Back-propagating Errors," popularizing backpropagation.

1987: AI Winter Begins

Funding cuts hit AI research as expert systems fail to meet expectations. The "AI Winter" lasts through the early 1990s.

1990: Neural Networks Return

Work on recurrent neural networks and learning algorithms brings neural networks back into focus.

1997: Deep Blue Beats Kasparov

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, marking a milestone in AI capability.

1998: LeNet-5

Yann LeCun introduces LeNet-5, a convolutional neural network for handwritten digit recognition.

Key Developments

  • First commercial AI applications
  • Machine learning as alternative to rule-based systems
  • Statistical approaches gain prominence
