2000s-2010s: Deep Learning Revolution

Neural networks achieve breakthrough results and AI becomes accessible to everyone

2006: Deep Learning Born

Geoffrey Hinton introduces "deep belief networks" and publishes "A Fast Learning Algorithm for Deep Belief Nets."

2009: GPUs for AI

Researchers at Stanford discover that GPUs can dramatically accelerate deep learning training, leading to the GPU computing revolution.

2012: ImageNet Breakthrough

AlexNet wins ImageNet competition with 85% accuracy, triggering the deep learning explosion. CNNs prove superior for image recognition.

2014: GANs

Ian Goodfellow introduces Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), enabling AI to create realistic images.

2015: TensorFlow

Google releases TensorFlow, making deep learning accessible to developers worldwide.

2016: AlphaGo

DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol, world champion at Go, using deep reinforcement learning.
