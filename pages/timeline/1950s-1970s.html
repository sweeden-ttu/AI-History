1950s-1970s: The Foundation Era

The birth of artificial intelligence and the pioneers who started it all

1950: Turing's Vision

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the famous Turing Test and establishing the fundamental question: Can machines think?

1956: Dartmouth Conference

The birth of AI as a field. John McCarthy coins the term "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on AI.

1957: Perceptron

Frank Rosenblatt creates the perceptron, the first artificial neural network, at Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory.

1966: ELIZA

Joseph Weizenbaum develops ELIZA at MIT, the first chatbot, demonstrating natural language processing.

1969: Backpropagation

Seppo Linnainmaa publishes the backpropagation algorithm, enabling efficient training of multi-layer neural networks.

1972: Prolog

Alain Colmerauer develops Prolog, a logic programming language that becomes foundational for AI research.
